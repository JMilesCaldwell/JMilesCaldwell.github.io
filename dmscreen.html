Conditions & Status Effects

These conditions modify a creature’s capabilities in a variety of ways and can arise as a result of a spell, a class feature, a monster’s attack, or other effect.

  • Blinded: Can’t see, automatically fails any check requiring sight, attack rolls against it have advantage.
  • Charmed: Can’t attack charmer, charmer has advantage on social checks.
  • Deafened: Can’t hear, automatically fails checks requiring hearing.
  • Frightened: Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is in line of sight.
  • Grappled: Speed becomes 0, ends if grappler is incapacitated or forced away.
  • Incapacitated: Can’t take actions or reactions.
  • Invisible: Cannot be seen without special sense or magic, advantage on attack rolls, attackers have disadvantage.
  • Paralyzed: Incapacitated, can’t move or speak, automatically fails Str/Dex saves, attacks against it have advantage, any hit is a critical hit if attacker is within 5 ft.
  • Petrified: Transformed into solid inanimate substance, weight increases 10x, ceases aging, incapacitated.
  • Poisoned: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
  • Prone: Movement requires half speed to stand, disadvantage on attack rolls, attackers have advantage if within 5 ft., else disadvantage.
  • Restrained: Speed 0, disadvantage on attack rolls and Dex saves, attacks against have advantage.
  • Stunned: Incapacitated, can’t move, can speak only falteringly, fails Str/Dex saves, attacks against have advantage.
  • Unconscious: Incapacitated, drops whatever it’s holding, falls prone, fails Str/Dex saves, attacks against have advantage, critical hits if attacker is within 5 ft.
  • Exhaustion: Six levels with progressively worse penalties, culminating in death at level 6.

Travel & Movement
  • Overland Travel Pace (PHB Default):
    • Fast: 4 miles/hour (penalty -5 Passive Perception)
    • Normal: 3 miles/hour (no penalty)
    • Slow: 2 miles/hour (can use Stealth)
  • Forced March: Each hour beyond 8 hours of travel requires a CON save (DC 10 + 1 per extra hour) or gain a level of exhaustion.
  • Visibility & Encounter Distance: Adjust for weather, terrain, time of day.

Damage & Healing
  • Falling Damage: 1d6 per 10 ft. (max 20d6).
  • Suffocating: Can hold breath for CON mod (min 30 seconds), then drops to 0 hp if runs out of breath/fails checks.
  • Resistance/Immunity/Vulnerability: Halves, negates, or doubles damage types.
  • Massive Damage: If damage reduces a creature to 0 hp and is massive enough to kill outright, the creature dies instantly.
  • Death Saving Throws: 3 successes stabilize, 3 failures = death; a 1 counts as two failures, 20 = regain 1 hp immediately.

Social Interactions

NPCs have attitudes based on circumstances and the party's approach:

  • Friendly: NPC wants to help if it can.
  • Indifferent: NPC offers no help but no hostility (persuasion or roleplay can shift attitude).
  • Hostile: NPC opposes the party actively or passively.

Use Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, or Insight checks to influence or glean motives. Adjust DCs for roleplay and context.
