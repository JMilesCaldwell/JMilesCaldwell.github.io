Mandala Geometry & Structure

The geometry follows the principle of the **Vajra Mandala** (Adamantine Circle), evolving from the outer layers of protection inward to the central deity.

1. Outer Protection Rings

  • Flame Ring: The outermost ring is a band of flames (derived from the Fire Element) symbolizing the burning away of ignorance.
  • Symbolic Fence: The second layer repeats the Central Symbol (e.g., OM, HRIH, Endless Knot), creating a **Vajra Fence** to ward off distraction and illusion.
  • Concentric Rings: A series of simple rings (alternating colors and styles) represent the accumulating layers of wisdom.

2. The Central Palace (Vajra Bhumi)

The inner structure is a large square palace with four distinct quarters, mapped to the cardinal directions and their associated colors/Buddha families. The palace is entered via four 'T' shaped gates (one for each direction):

  • The Four Quarters: Colored according to the element mapping (e.g., South-Yellow, West-Red, North-Green, East-White/Blue).
  • The Four T-Gates: Representing the opening of the senses toward enlightenment.

3. The Center Point

At the center of the palace is the central circle, the **Bindu** (point), which contains the **Seed Syllable** or **Auspice Symbol** (e.g., HUM, Lotus). This represents the mind of the **Yidam** (Meditation Deity) and the ultimate, unchanging state of reality.
