What is Śūnyatā?

Śūnyatā, often translated as “emptiness,” reflects the truth that all phenomena are interdependent, free from any separate, permanent essence. Far from nihilistic, it opens us to the infinite interconnectedness of all things.

  • Rooted in the concept of Dependent Origination.
  • Highlights the absence of intrinsic identity in phenomena.
  • Promotes clarity, compassion, and non-attachment.

The Four Noble Truths

1. Dukkha

Life includes dissatisfaction and suffering in various forms.

2. Samudaya

Craving and ignorance fuel the fires of suffering.

3. Nirodha

By extinguishing the causes, suffering can end—Nirvāṇa is possible.

4. Magga

The Noble Eightfold Path provides a practical route to liberation.
